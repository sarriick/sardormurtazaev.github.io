/ Research

Research interests

Algorithmic Discrimination in AI-Based Credit Scoring

Ongoing — MBA research, Sejong University

As machine learning models become embedded in lending decisions, questions arise about whether they encode or amplify historical biases — even without using protected attributes directly. My research examines proxy bias in credit features, applicable fairness metrics, and the regulatory landscape around automated financial decisions (EU AI Act, CFPB guidance).

This work connects a practical banking background with questions about responsible AI deployment in financial services and consumer protection.

Cashless Payments in Uzbekistan RU

Undergraduate publication — Tashkent State University of Economics

An examination of the development of non-cash payment instruments in Uzbekistan, analyzing trends in digital payment infrastructure, policy initiatives, and gaps in financial inclusion. Published as part of academic work at TSUE.
